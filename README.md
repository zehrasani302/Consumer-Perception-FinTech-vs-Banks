# FinTech vs Banks Dashboard

## Project Overview

This Power BI dashboard analyzes survey responses collected during my ELP (Final Year Project) at IBA.

The project compares customer perceptions of traditional banks and FinTech services in Pakistan, highlighting trends in digital payment adoption, trust, and user preferences.

---

## Objectives

- Compare FinTech and traditional banking preferences
- Analyze demographic trends
- Study digital payment adoption like PayPak
- Generate actionable insights using survey data

---

## Problem Statement

As digital financial services continue to grow, banks and FinTech companies compete for customer adoption. This project explores user behavior, awareness, security concerns, and payment preferences to identify opportunities for improving financial inclusion and digital payment adoption in Pakistan.

---

## Tools Used

- Power BI
- Microsoft Excel

---

## Key Insights

### Consumer Behavior
- 49% of respondents use traditional banks for bill payments, indicating a strong opportunity for banks to retain customers.
- FinTech applications were consistently rated higher for convenience, real-time transactions, and user experience.

### Security & Awareness
- Fear of scams and security breaches remains one of the biggest barriers to FinTech adoption.
- 61% of respondents were unaware of privacy risks associated with international payment schemes.

### Local Payment Schemes
- 82% of respondents had never heard of PayPak.
- Among those aware, most learned through friends, family, banks, or social media rather than advertising.
- Awareness of PayPak benefits remained low, with many respondents only associating it with Golootlo discounts.

### Market Trends
- Easypaisa significantly outperformed JazzCash in user preference.
- Debit cards remained the most common payment method for shopping, while QR code payments were mainly used by younger respondents.

### Strategic Recommendations
- Banks should enhance their digital experience to remain competitive.
- FinTech companies should focus on building trust through stronger security messaging.
- Greater public awareness campaigns are needed to improve adoption of Pakistan's local payment schemes.
